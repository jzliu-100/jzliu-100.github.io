Research Interests

My research mainly focuses on two strands of areas:
  • (1) socioeconomic policies relating to built environment and well-being, including urban air pollution, green space and mental health;
  • (2) computational methodologies and their use in urban analysis, including GIS, RS, visualization and big data analytics.

Profile

I am a Post-doctoral Fellow at the Department of Urban Planning and Design, Faculty of Architecture, The University of Hong Kong, and currently am visiting Department of Land Economy at The University of Cambridge. I work with Professor Chris Webster on a research project that links outdoor air pollution to mental health in China and UK settings. I hold a Bachelor of Science Degree in Geographical Information Science in East China Normal University, and a Master of Science Degree in Urban and Regional Planning from Peking University, and a Doctor of Philosophy degree at The Universit of Hong Kong.

My recent paper "Temporal Patterns in Fine Particulate Matter Time Series in Beijing: A Calendar View" published by Scientific Reports addresses a timely and important issue in China: Air pollution. The paper provided an innovative calendar visualization of PM2.5 measurements in Beijing throughout 2014 and concluded that there are three diurnal patterns and no weekly patterns; seasonal patterns exist but they do not follow a strict temporal division.

My full academic CV can be found here, and I am also on Twitter @JaymesLiu and WeChat (james_z_liu).

In my spare time, I serve as student Board member in International Association for China Planning (IACP) and I am in charge of IACP webportal and e-business. I am the founder of CNUrbanStudies.org, a free online Urban New Media focusing on China Urbanization and the first of its kind.
